BEST FIT

In this technique, operating system searches the whole memory for the holes that are capable to accomodate the process and then allocates the process to the closest fitting free hole. This technique enables efficient memory use.

Example: Below are the memory size required for allocation of 4 processes respectively.

Job 1 - 220k
Job 2 - 420k
Job 3 - 120k
Job 4 - 450k

Below are the memory size of 5 free holes or partitions respectively.

Block 1 - 100k
Block 2 - 500k
Block 3 - 200k
Block 4 - 300k
Block 5 - 600k

Job 1: Among the available free holes(block 2, 4, 5) that can allocate, Block 5 is the largest. Hence, job 1 is allocated to block 5.

Job 2: Among the available free holes(block 2) that can allocate, Block 2 is the largest. Hence, job 2 is allocated to block 2.

Job 3: - Among the available free holes(block 3, 4, internal fragment of 5) that can allocate, internal fragment of Block 5 is the largest. Hence, job 3 is allocated to block 5.

Advantages:
  • There will be large amount of internal fragmentation.
  • Sometimes, these internal fragments are large enough to allocate another process.

Disadvantages:
  • It is a slow process because it traverses all the partitions in the memory and then selects the largest among all the partitions.
