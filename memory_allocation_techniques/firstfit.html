FIRST FIT

First process claims the first available free hole with memory size more than or equal to the required space. The operating system does not search for appropriate partition but just allocate the job to the nearest memory partition available with sufficient size.

Example: Below are the memory size required for allocation of 5 processes respectively.

Job 1 - 90k
Job 2 - 20k
Job 3 - 50k
Job 4 - 10k
Job 5 - 100k

Below are the memory size of 5 free holes or partitions respectively.

Block 1 - 50k
Block 2 - 100k
Block 3 - 90k
Block 4 - 200k
Block 5 - 50k

Job 1: 50k < 90k - Itcannot be allocated to block 1

100k>90k - job 1 is allocated to block 2

Job 2: 20k < 50k - job 2 is allocated to block 1

Job 3: 50k < 90k - job 3 is allocated to block 3

Job 4: 10k < 200k - job 4 is allocated to block 4

Job 5: It cannot be allocated due to lack of space.

Advantages:
  • It is a fast process as this technique does not require searching.

Disadvantages:
  • There is a possibility of wastage of memory.
  • The processor just allocates the process without verifying whether the hole is very large compared to the required memory space.
